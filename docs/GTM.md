## Positioning To Test

Primary message:

> **A Mac focus anchor that nudges you back when you drift.**

Supporting line:

> For ADHD-aware knowledge workers who lose the thread mid-session.

Privacy line:

> No screenshots. No keystrokes. Opt-in active app/window detection only.

Avoid:

- "treats ADHD",
- "clinically validated",
- "AI coach",
- "body doubling network",
- "always-on copilot",
- "built for every ADHD brain."

The point of validation is to test whether users want a narrow Mac utility, not whether a broad ADHD platform can be marketed.

## 2-Week Channel Plan

### 1. Direct outreach

Highest-signal channel for validation.

- Send 30 targeted messages to Mac-using knowledge workers with ADHD-aware productivity pain.
- Ask for a 20-minute interview, not a sale.
- Target mix: PMs, designers, engineers, founders, writers, consultants.

Goal: 10 completed interviews.

### 2. Founder social posts

Post 3 short pieces on X/LinkedIn:

- "I thought the Mac ADHD focus lane was empty. It is not. Here is the sharper problem I am testing."
- "Do you lose the thread mid-session? I am testing a Mac focus anchor with opt-in drift nudges."
- 30-second prototype/mockup clip.

Goal: 50 qualified landing visits.

### 3. Reddit/community participation

Use Reddit only for learning during validation.

- Leave thoughtful comments where relevant.
- Do not make promotional r/ADHD launch posts.
- Ask for interview volunteers only where community rules allow.

Goal: 3-5 interviews or high-quality objections.

### 4. Micro-creator feedback

Contact 3 small ADHD/productivity creators for feedback on the concept, not paid promotion.

Goal: identify language that resonates and objections that would hurt trust.

## Fake-Door Landing Page

Required sections:

- Headline: "A Mac focus anchor that nudges you back when you drift"
- Problem: task initiation, mid-session drift, and lost re-entry context
- How it works: capture task, keep it visible, nudge on drift, save re-entry note
- Privacy: no screenshots, no keystrokes, opt-in app/window detection, pause anytime
- Competitor-aware copy: not a blocker, planner, coach, or body-doubling app
- CTA: "Join the beta"
- Optional CTA: "Reserve early access" for paid-intent testing

Lead form fields:

- email,
- primary work role,
- Mac as primary device yes/no,
- biggest pain: start, drift, re-entry, time blindness, hyperfocus,
- would you pay for this if it worked? yes/no/maybe.

## Metrics

| Metric | Go threshold |
|---|---:|
| Qualified waitlist signups | 200 |
| Paid/beta commitments | 20 |
| Completed interviews | 10 |
| Interviewees accepting opt-in drift detection | 70% |
| Interviewees ranking problem top-3 weekly pain | 70% |
| Users choosing Focusly concept over direct alternatives | 50% |

Qualified means:

- uses Mac for work,
- does knowledge work 20+ hours/week,
- reports task initiation, drift, or re-entry pain,
- is willing to try a Mac utility.

## Pricing Test

Do not assume $9.99/mo.

Test:

- $29 lifetime early-access,
- $79 lifetime standard,
- $5-$10 monthly,
- free beta with optional paid deposit.

Reason: adjacent Mac competitors include low annual and lifetime pricing, so subscription willingness must be validated before billing infrastructure is built.

## Decision Outcomes

**Go:** Build narrow MVP if demand, commitment, and codebase gates pass.

**Iterate:** Run another validation cycle if interviews show real pain but positioning or trust is weak.

**No-Go:** Stop the ADHD-Mac pivot if users do not commit, reject monitoring, or see competitors as good enough.
